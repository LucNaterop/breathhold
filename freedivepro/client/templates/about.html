Notes on CO2 Tolerance and O2 Deprivation

CO2 Tolerance

Carbon dioxide (CO2) tolerance breath/hold intervals will help your body to become tolerant to high levels of CO2 in the blood. It is important to remember that the urge to breathe is caused by high levels of CO2, and not low levels of O2. If you build up CO2 tolerance, the urge will come in later.


O2 Deprivation

Oxygen (O2) deprivation breath/hold intervals will help your body deal with low levels of O2. When holding your breath, oxygen (O2) levels in your blood start to lower. It is therefore immensely important that you train your body to use very little oxygen when holding your breath. In these sessions, you make your body familiar to low levels of O2, to which he will respond by actually using less oxygen.


General remarks

O2 deprivation and CO2 tolerance tables are being automatically generated. In addition, this app gives you the possibility to create your own completely customized tables.
